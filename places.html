WHERE I'VE BEEN

It is a great pleasure for me to experience education in four continents. I was born and spent 12 years of elementary and high schools in Vietnam. I then earned a bachelor degree in New Zealand before pursuing a Ph.D. degree in the U.S. Currently I am a postdoctoral researcher in Norway.

ASIA

I was born in My Tho city. This is the capital city of Tien Giang province, located in the Mekong River Delta region of the southern Vietnam. My Tho is popular for hu tieu, a type of noodle soup, as well as other specialties in the Mekong River Delta region such as boat rides, Don ca tai tu---a genre of chamber music---tropical fruits (star apple, durian, mango, longan, dragon fruit, etc.).

AUSTRALIA

In February 2011 I travelled to New Zealand for my undergraduate study at Victoria University of Wellington. The university is in the capital city, Wellington. The city is very compact. You can walk around the downtown within one day. Yet there are a lot of special foods and activities to enjoy. The weather is wonderful except the wind. The worst day is not a winter day but a raindy and windy day. There's a favourite quote "You can't beat Wellington on a windy day." Nobody hence uses an umbrella here. It isn't supposed to have snow in Wellington, but I was lucky to see a rare snow in 2011.
